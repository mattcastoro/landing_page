Common bird species found in my neighborhood
On this site you will find a few common birds found near my house: on feeders, in the grass, on fences, and of course, in the air. Sign up for our bird of the week newsletter - it lands each Sunday.
Popular birds one can easily find...

Black-billed Magpie - Large, conspicuious, black and white bird; related to crows and jays. Unmistakably long tail and bold pattern, especially visible in flight. Often spotted along roadsides in open country, fields, and towns. Social and inquisitive, often in small groups foraging on the ground.

Northern Flicker - Large, brownish woodpecker with black barring on the back and black spots on the belly. Easily recognized in flight by its bright white rump. Also note large black crescent-shaped mark on breast. Wings and tail flash yellow or red, depending on subspecies. Often seen feeding on the ground in open areas, foraging for ants and worms.

Steller's Jay - Black and blue with a crest. Widespread in western North America, from Alaska to Nicaragua. Shows marked variation across range: coastal populations are the darkest, with no pale markings around the eye. Occurs in coniferous forests, usually in small groups. Regulary visits feeders, campgrounds, and picnic areas. Loud and conspicuious; listen for harsh calls.

American Kestral - Petite falcon roughly the same size as a Mourning Dove, but with a larger head and wider tail. In flight, note long, narrow wings and square-tipped tail. Oten seen perched on telphone wires, along roadsides, in open country with short vegetation and few trees. From a perch or hovering, they usually drop to the ground to snatch small mammals and insects. Next in cavities.
